First Aid for Epilepsy

Knowing how to respond to a seizure can help ensure the safety of someone experiencing one. Here's what you need to know about providing first aid for epilepsy.

Steps to Take During a Seizure

  • 1. Stay Calm: Remain composed and ensure the individual is safe.
  • 2. Protect from Injury: Gently guide the person to the ground and move objects away to prevent harm.
  • 3. Cushion the Head: Place a soft item, such as a folded jacket, under their head.
  • 4. Time the Seizure: Note the start time and duration of the seizure.
  • 5. Do Not Restrict Movement: Allow the seizure to run its course without holding the person down.
  • 6. Do Not Put Anything in the Mouth: This can cause choking or injury.
  • 7. Roll onto the Side: After the seizure, gently roll the person onto their side to help with breathing.
  • 8. Stay with Them: Offer reassurance and stay until they are fully alert.

When to Call for Emergency Help

Call emergency services if:

  • The seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes.
  • The person does not regain consciousness after the seizure.
  • The person has multiple seizures without recovering between them.
  • The seizure occurs in water.
  • It is their first known seizure.

Myths About Seizures

Avoid common misconceptions:

  • You cannot swallow your tongue during a seizure.
  • Do not restrain someone during a seizure.
  • Seizures are not contagious.
